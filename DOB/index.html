About us

“Which of my photographs is my favourite? The one I’m going to take tomorrow.” – Imogen Cunningham

I am Krishna chaithanya, a Lithuanian maternity and baby photographer, settled in one of the finest Indian cities – Pune. I am just another hard working, creative mommy, whose hobby turned into an exciting journey and a fascinating career – #DOB Photography.

My inspiration is Life – the very beginning of it in the womb and beautiful flourishing of each birth. My passion – to capture this magical proof of continuity via clicks of amazing Moms, fragile newborns and champion children.

The thing I love most about my Job – wonderful people I meet every day, who are the most important, creative and combining elements of each photo-shoot and my personal success.

Cheers to you all!

FAQ

How to book an appointment?

You may schedule an appointment with us by calling our studio on +919573796339 or you can fill the adjacent appointment form to request an appointment online one of our team members will get in touch with you.

What are maternity and baby photo shoot packages?

You can find our range of maternity, newborn, baby, child photography pricing & packages here. The photo shoot packages start from INR 25 000. If you have a specific requirement you’d like to discuss, we’re more than happy to help you find the most suitable and cost effective photo session for your needs, just contact us.

How to book a photo session?

To book a date for a photo session, please call +919573796339 to find a date which is available as well as suitable for you. To confirm the date an advance payment is required. Once these formalities are done, the photographer will consult you about clothing and other details. The consultation can be done in person at the studio or over the phone.

How many days in advance should i book my photo session?

We suggest booking two weeks in advance. We try our best to accommodate any last minute bookings, but to ensure you get your preferred time and date, we recommend booking 4-5 weeks in advance.
